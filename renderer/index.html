How to get the most out of Filament Vault
How it works

Filament Vault uses two things together:

  • A Catalog — a list of every filament you own or might own, identified by UPC barcode. Think of it as your filament "dictionary."
  • Your Inventory — the actual spools you have right now, with counts.

You scan or enter your spools' barcodes, the app looks them up in the catalog, and your inventory builds itself automatically.

Step 1 — Import your Catalog

Before scanning spools, you need a catalog so the app knows what each barcode means.

  • Go to Import and open the Filament Catalog card.
  • Upload a CSV file with columns: UPC, Brand, Material, Type, Color, Name
  • You can download a sample catalog to see the exact format.
  • Re-importing is safe — it merges new entries in and never deletes existing inventory.
💡 Bambu filament catalogs are available as community CSV files online. You only need to import once, then keep it updated as new filaments release.
Step 2 — Add your Spools

Once your catalog is loaded, go to Import → UPC Scan or Paste and choose a method:

  • Hand scanner (fastest) — pair a Bluetooth barcode scanner, tap "Paste UPCs or Use Scanner," click in the box, then scan each spool. Hit Process when done. Buy a compatible scanner on Amazon ↗
  • Paste UPCs — type or paste a list of UPCs, one per line, and hit Process.
  • Upload a file — if your scanner exports a .txt file, use the Upload File tab.
  • Manual entry — scroll down to Add Spool Manually, type a UPC and quantity, and hit Add.
Each time a UPC is processed it adds +1 spool. Scanning the same UPC twice means you have two spools — that's intentional.
The Inventory view

Your main view. Each entry shows:

  • Color swatch — visual colour chip
  • Name, Brand, Material, Type — from your catalog
  • Spool count — tap + or − to adjust as you use filament
  • Location — tap to set where this filament lives (e.g. Shelf A, Dry Box 2)
  • ★ Essential — star a filament you always want in stock. Use Essential Only to focus on just these.
⚠️ Any filament with only 1 spool left shows a low-stock warning and a red count.
The Catalog view
  • Browse your full filament database
  • Use the Not in Inventory tab to find catalog entries you haven't scanned yet
  • Edit entries to fix names, colours, or barcodes
  • Star filaments as Essential even before you have them in stock
Sorting & Filtering (mobile)

Use the sort pills above the card list to sort by Spools, Name, Color, Brand, or Material. Tap the same pill again to flip between high→low and low→high.

Unlinked Barcodes

If you edit or delete a catalog entry, inventory items that referenced it lose their link. This section shows those items and lets you re-link them to the correct catalog entry or clean them up.

Unmatched Scans

If you scan a UPC that doesn't exist in your catalog, it lands here. You can link it to an existing catalog entry, create a new entry for it, or delete it if it was a mis-scan.

Syncing across devices

Sign in with Google to sync your inventory to the cloud. Any device you log into will see the same data — great for scanning in the workshop on your phone and checking stock on your desktop.

Your data is stored securely. If you're not signed in, data is saved locally in your browser only.
Exporting your data

Go to More → Export Inventory (mobile) or Export Inventory in the sidebar (desktop) to download a full CSV of your current stock.

📱 Adding to your iPhone home screen

For the best mobile experience, install Filament Vault as an app:

  1. Open this page in Safari (does not work in Chrome on iOS)
  2. Tap the Share button at the bottom of the screen
  3. Tap "Add to Home Screen"
  4. Tap Add in the top-right corner

It launches full-screen like a native app with no browser bars.

🔍 Database diagnostics

Shows every save and load operation with Supabase. Useful for troubleshooting data sync issues.
